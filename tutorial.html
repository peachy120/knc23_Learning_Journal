Ingredients

    • Cucumber
    • Egg
    • beef
    • Nori
    • Rice

Seasoning

    • 1/2 teaspoon - Salt
    • Oil
    • Light Soy Sauce
    • Sugar

Utensils

    • Frying pan
    • Spatula
    • Plasic wrap
    • Chopping board
    • Knife

Instructions

  1. Step 1

    The rice takes the longest time to prepare, so it is best to cook the rice at the begining.

    When the rice is cooked, lay it out flat on a plate or any surface to let it cool down and leave it aside.

  2. Step 2

    Chop the Cucumber into slices first. Then shred it and leave it aside for later use.

  3. Step 3

    Crack the eggs and beat until the white and yolk are well mixed. Also add in the salt.

    Add oil into a pan and heat it up until you can feel the heat with your hand placing above the pan. Pour in the egg mixture and spread it allover the pan.

    When it is totally cooked, fold it in half or in thirds. Then place it on a plate for cooling down.

    When it is cooled down, shred it and leave it aside for later use.

  4. Step 4

    Heat up the pan again and wait until it is hot, place the beef on the frying pan and smash it with the Spatula.

    Add in the light soy sauce and i+mix well. Cook uniil all the beaf turn brown and place it on a plate for cooling down.

  5. Step 5

    When all the food inside the is pre-prepared and cooldown already. Place a piece of plastic wrap on top of a clean chopping board.

    Place a piece of nori on top.

  6. Step 6

    Start by putting the rice, shred egg, cooked beef and shred cucumber.

    Place the rice on the nori first, fill the 3/4 of the seaween. Leave some space on one of the end foreasier wrapping.

    Then put in the other ingredients in.

    Note: DO NOT put to much ingredients or else it will be difficult to wrap all the ingredients in.

    Note: To prevent the rice sticking on your hand and not the nori, put some water on your hand before taking and spreading the rice on the nori.

  7. Step 7

    With the help of the plastic wrap, use your fingers to press onto the filling while rolling to prevent the fillings from dropping out.

    Put in some pressure with your hands to let the rice stick together until it rooled into a roll.

    When it forms into a roll, use the plastic wrap to wrap it tight and plae it into the fridge for a few hours so it can be harded a bit.

    It will be easier to cut into slices as it is slightly hidden.

  8. Step 8

    Take the Gimbap roll out from the fridge and place it on a chopping board.

    Cut out each Gimbap roll and place it on a plate. It is ready to eat.

    Note: It is optional to add some sesame oil on top.
